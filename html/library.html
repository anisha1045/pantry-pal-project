Micronutrients vs Macronutrients

Understanding the two major nutrient groups:

  • Macronutrients: Nutrients needed in large amounts to provide energy and build body structures.
    • • Main types: 1. Carbohydrates — the primary energy source; broken down into glucose. 2. Proteins — build and repair tissues, enzymes, hormones. 3. Fats — energy storage, cell structure, hormone production. 4. Water (sometimes considered a macro because needed in large amounts, though it provides no energy). 5. Fiber is sometimes grouped with carbs but it doesn’t provide energy directly; it supports digestion. • Role: Supply calories, support growth, maintain body tissues, and regulate body processes.
  • Micronutrients: Nutrients needed in smaller amounts but essential for proper body functions and disease prevention.
    • • Main types: • Vitamins (e.g., A, B-complex, C, D, E, K) — organic compounds necessary for numerous biochemical functions. • Minerals (e.g., iron, calcium, magnesium, zinc, potassium, sodium, phosphorus) — inorganic elements vital for bone formation, oxygen transport, nerve signaling, enzyme activity. • Energy: Micronutrients do NOT provide energy (calories). • Role: • Help convert macronutrients into energy. • Support immune system. • Maintain healthy bones and teeth. • Aid in blood clotting, oxygen transport, antioxidant functions, and hormone production.
  • Macronutrients include: Calories, protein, fat, carbs, fiber
  • Micronutrients include: Vitamins A, B6, B12, C, D, E, K, iron, calcium, magnesium, zinc, potassium, sodium, phosphorus
